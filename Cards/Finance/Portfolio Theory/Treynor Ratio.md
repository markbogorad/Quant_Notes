# Treynor Ratio
- Excess return per unit of risk in comparison to risk free asset
- Uses systematic risk instead of total risk (in contrast to [[Sharpe Ratio]])
- The higher the ratio, the better
$$ {Treynor Ratio} = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\beta_p} $$
- **Rp:** Portfolio return
- **Rf:** Risk free rate
- **Bp:** Portfolio beta
